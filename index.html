01 — The Problem

DDoS attacks are evolving.
Detection tools haven't kept up.

Conventional IDS tools examine each network connection independently — like trying to identify a coordinated assault by inspecting individual footsteps. They miss the temporal pattern.

Research Question: Can temporal deep learning models that analyze sequences of network flows detect DDoS attacks — including ambiguous "Suspicious" traffic — with high accuracy on the BCCC-Cloud-DDoS-2024 dataset?

02 — Our Approach

Temporal IDS Pipeline

Instead of classifying individual flows, we group 30 consecutive time-sorted flows into windows and train two complementary models.

05 — Final Steps & Challenges

What's left to do

Known Limitations
  • Suspicious precision ~70% — roughly 3 in 10 Suspicious alerts are false positives. Mitigated by the escalation threshold.
  • Single dataset — trained and tested on BCCC only. Generalization to other networks is untested.
  • Fixed 30-flow window — may miss attacks that are shorter or longer than this span.
